Our Services

WHAT WE TREAT

Wound Care
Pressure Ulcers
Skin breakdown caused by prolonged pressure, common in patients with limited mobility or those confined to bed.
Diabetic Ulcers
Wounds resulting from poor circulation and nerve damage due to diabetes, typically occurring on the feet.
Venous Ulcers
Open sores caused by blood pooling in the veins, usually appearing on the lower legs near the ankle.
Arterial Ulcers
Wounds from reduced blood flow to the extremities — often on the feet, toes, and outer ankles.
Infected Wounds
Wounds where bacterial contamination impedes the natural healing process, requiring targeted treatment.
Chronic & Post-Surgical Wounds
Wounds failing to heal within 4 weeks, or post-surgical wounds past 90 days needing continued professional care.
Burns & Traumatic Wounds
Tissue damage from heat, chemicals, or physical trauma requiring specialized wound management protocols.
Internal Medicine
Primary Care
Chronic Care Management
Ongoing management of long-term conditions to improve quality of life and prevent complications.
Diabetes
Blood sugar monitoring, medication management, and education to control Type 1 & Type 2 diabetes.
Hypertension
High blood pressure monitoring and treatment to reduce the risk of stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease.
Heart Disease
Coordinated cardiac care including medication review, lifestyle guidance, and specialist collaboration.
Pulmonary Disorders
Management of COPD, asthma, and other respiratory conditions to improve breathing and daily function.

Do I Need a Referral?

We aim to schedule your first home visit within 24–72 hours of insurance approval and referral receipt.

Our clinicians follow CDC guidelines and use proper PPE during all home visits to keep you and your family safe.

Yes — we provide wound care at homes, nursing homes, and assisted living facilities across our service counties.
